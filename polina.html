Bio

Polina Anikeeva was born in Leningrad (USSR) and grew up in St. Petersburg, Russia. She received her B.S. in Biophysics from St. Petersburg State Polytechnic University in 2003, and spent time as a researcher at Los Alamos National Laboratory working on semiconductor nanocrystal solar cells. She completed her Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering at MIT in 2009, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship in Neuroscience and Bioengineering at Stanford University.

Polina founded the Bioelectronics Group at MIT in 2011 and achieved tenure in 2017. On July 1, 2024, she was named Head of MIT's Department of Materials Science and Engineering (DMSE), succeeding Caroline Ross. Her research program combines the current knowledge of biology and nanoelectronics to develop materials and devices for minimally invasive treatments of neurological and neuromuscular diseases.
